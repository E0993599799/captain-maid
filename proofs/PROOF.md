## 9. Known limitations (stated plainly, not fudged)

1. **No real Safari or iOS device was tested, at all.** WebKit-via-Playwright — the closest available proxy — could not even launch in this sandbox (see §7). This report contains zero WebKit/Safari-engine runtime evidence.
2. **No real physical mobile device** (Android or iOS) was used for any screenshot or interaction — all viewport testing is Chromium's device emulation (viewport resize), not a real touchscreen or real mobile GPU/network.
3. **No live Lighthouse CI dashboard / historical trend** — this is a single point-in-time local run, not wired into any CI pipeline (none exists in this monorepo yet).
4. **Skeleton-screen / optimistic-UI verification is N/A**, per the task's own framing: this site is almost entirely static/SSG (confirmed by the build output — 20/20 pages prerendered, only `/api/revalidate` and `/products` are dynamic) with no client-side data-loading states to verify.
5. **The mobile-simulated Lighthouse Home-page numbers (43/100, 34s LCP) are real but should not be read in isolation** — see §8a's diagnostic breakdown. Investigating/fixing the hero-image weight is out of scope for this proof pipeline (would require touching home section files, explicitly disallowed by this task's constraints).
6. **This machine is a shared, loaded WSL2 VM**, not a clean benchmarking rig — another agent's `codex` sandbox process was observed running concurrently during parts of this session. Lighthouse scores can be sensitive to host contention; the desktop-preset numbers (§8b) are offered as a lower-noise cross-check for this reason, not as the "real" score to the exclusion of §8a.
7. **`next lint` deprecation warning** and a "multiple lockfiles detected, workspace root inferred" warning appear in lint/build output — both are pre-existing monorepo-wide conditions (multiple sibling projects under `/mnt/d/.../mission-control` with their own lockfiles), not something introduced by or fixable within this task's scope.
